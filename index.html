    Freddie Mercury timeline

  • 1946 (5th September) Freddie Mercury was born Farrokh Bulsara to Bomi and Jer Bulsara in Stone Town, Zanzibar (Tanzania). His father worked as a cashier at the British Colonial Office
  • 1954 Freddie was sent to St Peter’s Boarding School in Panchgani, Bombay, India.
  • 1958 Freddie formed ‘The Hectics’ a school band that played covers of popular songs. Around this time he began to use the christian name Freddie.
  • 1964 The Zanzibar Revolution forced Freddie and his family to leave Zanzibar. They fled to England where they lived in Feltham.
  • 1966 Freddie studied Art and Design at Ealing Art College.
  • 1968 Freddie became friends with art student Tim Staffell who, along with astonomy student Brian May had formed the group Smile.
  • 1969 Freddie joined Liverpool band ‘Ibex’ and moved to Liverpool where he lived in a flat.
  • 1970 When Freddie’s friend, Tim Staffell, left the group Smile, Freddie Bulsara persuaded Brian May and group drummer Roger Taylor that he should replace Staffell as lead singer.
  • 1970 Encouraged by Freddie, the band Smile changed their name to Queen.
  • 1971 Queen played as supporting act for the group Yes at Kingston Polytechnic.
  • 1971 Freddie Balsara changed his name to Freddie Mercury.
  • 1972 Queen signed a recording contract with Trident Studios. They were to be managed by Neptune Productions, a subsidiary of Trident, and would record new material at Trident Studios.
  • 1973 Queen released their first album entitled ‘Queen’. It reached number 83 in the US and number 47 in the UK.
  • 1974 The group began a tour of America as supporting act to Mott The Hoople.
  • 1975Queen played for the first time in Canada
  • 1975 (October) The group wanted to release ‘Bohemian Rhapsody’, written by Freddie, as a single but they were told that at 5 minutes and 55 seconds no radio station would play it and it would be a flop. Freddie Mercury gave a copy of the single to his friend, DJ Kenny Everett, telling him he was not to play it but Everett did play it and other radio stations followed suit.
  • 1976 Freddie Mercury received an Ivor Novello Award for ‘Bohemian Rhapsody’.
  • 1977 The single ‘We Are The Champions’ with ‘We Will Rock You’ as the B side, was released. It reached number 2 in the UK and number 4 in the US. Both tracks were taken from the album ‘News Of The World’.
  • 1979 Queen performed at the Concert for the People of Kampuchea in London which had been organised as a charity event by Paul McCartney.
  • 1981 The group began a tour of South America, the first rock group to tour the continent.
  • 1984 The song ‘I Want to Break Free’ from the album ‘The Works’ was released as a single. It reached number 3 in the UK and number 45 in the US.
  • 1987 Freddie Mercury was diagnosed with AIDS. The diagnosis was kept from the public.
  • 1988 Freddie Mercury and Montserrat Caballe performed ‘Barcelona’ in Barcelona to celebrate the arrival of the Olympic flag from Seoul.
  • 1990 Freddie Mercury denied rumours that he was very ill.
  • 1991 (June) Freddie retired from public life to his home in Kensington. He was cared for by Jim Hutton and former girlfriend and lifelong friend, May Austin.
  • 1991 (23rd November) Freddie Mercury publicly announced that he had AIDS.
  • 1991 (24th November) Freddie Mercury died of bronchial pneumonia which he had contracted as a complication of AIDS.
  • 1991 (27th November) After a funeral service at West London Crematorium, Freddie’s friend, May Austin took his ashes and buried them at an unknown location.
